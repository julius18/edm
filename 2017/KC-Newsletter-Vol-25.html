WHEN IT COMES TO WINTER SUN, THIS FABLED THAI ISLAND IS HARD TO BEAT



As the cold weather sets in across northern Europe and America, for many it's the perfect time to plan that winter break - and when it comes to winter sun, the island of Phuket in southern Thailand is hard to beat. While the north freezes over, this legendary tropical island basks in delicious sunshine, its white beaches lapped by the turquoise waters of the Gulf of Thailand. But of course there's more than just picture postcard beaches to Southeast Asia's favourite tourist destination. Phuket is a foodie's paradise - boasting some of the best street food stalls in the world as well as fine dining renditions of the country's famous cuisine. Then there's the vibrant nightlife, island hopping day trips taking in the famous Phang Nga Bay, world class diving and snorkelling and of course Thailand's rich cultural tapestry - from Buddhist temples to the Phuket's historic old town. Set along Phuket's quaint Marina, Karma Royal Phuket's smart 2 bedroom apartments gaze out across the bobbing boats in the harbor and are served by a pool, gym, spa, games room, library and restaurants serving Thai, French and Italian. Where better to escape the winter blues for a couple of unforgettable weeks?

ALONG THE SILK ROUTE, A MAJESTIC ODE TO CHINA'S
SPLENDID DYNASTIES

The sheer grandeur of the Silk Road Dunhuang hotel is something that immediately strikes you. It's setting amid the Mingsha Sand Dunes in China's northwestern Gansu province, famed for its starkly beautiful landscapes, accentuates its elegant architecture, inspired by the Tang Dynasty. Inside, fixtures and furnishings hark back to the Ming and Qing dynasties. The Silk Road itself is of course one of the historically most significant trade routes in the world - and one that China is currently reviving. Discover more about its history while indulging in the opulence of one of China's finest resorts - fully equipped with the latest contemporary facilities, including a host of leisure facilities, spa with sauna and steam room, golf and on site restaurants.

DISCOVER AN OASIS AMID THE HIGH ALTITUDE RAINFORESTS OF BOLIVIA

The Rio Selva Resort is just under 50 miles from the Bolivian capital La Paz, but a world away in terms of environment. La Paz is the world's highest capital at nearly 12,000 feet - Rio Selva is just a quarter of that - but still relatively high altitude for rain forest. Here, a multitude of species converge around the crystal clear waterways in the Yungas jungle. The Inca and Choro trails pass through here and you'll definitely want to explore the resort's spectacular surroundings. Relax in air-conditioned comfort, enjoy a game of tennis or bask in the Jacuzzi. Be sure to bring some of the area's famous coffee with you when you leave!
