The Spirit of Goa & Kerala

The neighbouring states of Goa & Kerala may not be India's largest, but what they lack for in size they make up for in diversity, beauty and culture. Goa, with its 500 year Portuguese colonial history offers a rich mix of native & hybrid architecture, cultural practices and religious iconography, while Kerala has a similarly rich colonial history as a centre of the spice trade. While both are rightly famed for their incredible expanses of white, palm fringed sand, their interiors are equally captivating - from Kerala's meandering inland waterways that can be explored on traditional house boats, to Goa's richly endowed national parks, home to some of the highest levels of biodiversity in the subcontinent. Both are among the most enticing destinations on the planet.
