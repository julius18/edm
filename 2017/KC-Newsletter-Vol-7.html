GOING GREEK - THE HEARTLAND OF MEDITERRANEAN COOKING


Mediterranean cuisine is often touted as among the healthiest in the world. That may be true, but what is certain is that it's delicious. Mediterranean of course covers a multitude of different traditions, but the Greek is among the most celebrated. Greek cooking makes the most of what's available locally - the country produces the best olive oil in the world and is famed for its mezze dishes - the Greek equivalent of tapas. Think Dolmades (stuffed vine leaves), spanakopita (spinach & feta pie) and of course hummus. Meze dishes are best served with an aperitif and yes you guessed it, that drink would be ouzo! Each Greek region has its own drinks however - in the north it is Tsipouro, in Crete, Raki. Everyday food places a great emphasis on legumes, healthy grains, fish, yoghurt and meat all of which make for one of the most varied and delicious cuisines in the world!

KARMA CÂY TRE, Hoi An, Vietnam

Last edition, we introduced Vietnam as a brand new Southeast Asian destination for Karma Club members. Now we're ready to share more about the resort. It's called Karma Cây Tre - a collection of standalone villa suites spread across 10,000 square metres of verdant gardens on the banks of the serene Do River. Here simplicity and understated elegance combine to create a soothing ambience, where comfort is paramount. Each of the 22 rooms boasts a private balcony and many overlook the purling river. Head to the semi al fresco courtyard restaurant, which serves delicious renditions of classic Vietnamese dishes as well as international fare. Refresh yourself in the expansive pool, enjoy specially prepared barbecues riverside and discover the secrets of the local cuisines with farm tours and cooking classes. Karma Cây Tre is your gateway to one of southeast Asia's most exciting and up and coming destinations.

DISCOVER BALI'S UNIQUE CULTURE AT THE SANUR VILLAGE FESTIVAL

Next month is high season on the fabled island of Bali - a time for festivities, with a big focus on showcasing Bali's unique cultural offerings. Over the years the Sanur Village festival has grown into one of island's finest. It's a great opportunity to sample traditional food, witness classical dance and music performances by accomplished gamelan bands, as well as engaging with more contemporary happenings like fashions shows, yoga and the festival's Creative Expo. Don't miss the international kite festival, where scores of enormous handmade kites are sent aloft - plus the jukung festival where local fishing boats are adorned with stunning decorations. Think of it as the best of Bali distilled in one amazing celebration!
