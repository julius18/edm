Festive February in Phuket



Long the jewel of the Andaman Sea, Phuket teems with stunning vistas, amazing nightlife and the beating heart of Thai culture. By day, take in oceanic wonders with world-class diving and tours, traditional Thai fishing boat trips out to the Andaman Islands, or peaceful eco-tour walks underneath tropical rainforest canopies. By night, dine on fresh-caught seafood, or step out to marvel at some of Phuket's more colourful nightlife.

This 3rd-5th February, don't miss out on the Phuket Old Town Festival - an amazing cultural affirmation that follows Chinese New Year celebrations across the region. Sample amazing street food as you cruise by boat Phuket's central canals; catch a Chinese Dragon dance at Queen Sirikit Park or marvel at a Phuket Baba Light Show.

Spooky adventures in Bali

The first three months of 2017 are a truly amazing time to be in Bali, Indonesia. Well known for its colourful and deeply spiritual culture, the Balinese calendar culminates on the 27th and 28th of March.

On the evening of the 27th of March, villages and streets across the island come alive with raucous cheers and weird demons in the form of Ogoh-ogoh, grotesque large statues built for the Ngrupuk parade, which takes place on the eve of Nyepi day, often referred to as Balinese New Year or the "Day of Silence". Young men from each spend weeks carving and painting the Ogoh-Ogoh, designed to represent mythological beings, mainly demons.

Catalan cool in Barcelona

You may think because Barcelona - one of the world's great cities - sits on the coast of the Balearic Sea, that it's a summertime city. While it's true that the Catalonians turn on the summertime charm, there's something to be said for sitting street side along Las Ramblas, sipping a hefty tempranillo and dining on a daytime tapas of Escalivada, Botifarra negra and Pa amb tomàquet.

After lunch, explore the Museu Picasso, the most comprehensive collection of Picasso's early works anywhere, or stand before Antoni Gaudí's living dreamscape, the Sagrada Família - surely one of the most stunning architectural creations every created.

Mauritius a nature-lover's paradise

The all year round island paradise of Mauritius is a must-visit destination for lovers of sun, sand and nature. Located approximately 2000 kilometres south-west off the African continent, this small island nation punches well above its weight when it comes to amazing sights.

Visitors will be stunned speechless by scene of azure waters lapping along white sand shores and the island's unique geology. No visit would be complete without a The Seven Coloured Earths - a truly awe inspiring geological formation, where sands of seven different colours settle randomly to create an ever-changing tapestry of colour.
