MARCH OF THE PENGUINS - FOR A GREAT CAUSE



Philanthropy has always been at the heart of Karma Group's operations and we support a wide range of causes and charities. Last year John Spence Karma Group Chairman decided to add a light-hearted yet important string to Karma Group's charitable bow by setting up the annual Penguin Walk, where volunteers dress up as penguins and do a walk to raise money for good causes. The inaugural walk in 2015 saw Karma volunteers walking 38km through Provence, from 6am to 3pm, arriving at Club 55 on Pamplona Beach just in time for their lunch reservation! This year, the crew dressed up as kangaroos, aiming to beat last year's funding goal of £6700...the event was a great success and over £10,000 has already been raised to support the children of Christel House, Bangalore, India. It's still not too late to donate - head over to www.justgiving.com/fundraising/Karma-Penguin-Walk. To find out more about our charitable programmes or get involved, contact your Karma Concierge.

BAVARIA - AN ALPINE WONDERLAND WITH ITS OWN UNIQUE CHARACTER

Award-winning Karma Bavaria is the perfect base from which to explore the breathtaking beauty of the Bavarian Alps, its historic towns & cities and hearty country cuisine. Set on the shores of Lake Schliersee at the foot of the Wendelstein mountains Karma Bavaria offers self contained apartments and bright, cosy hotel rooms, indulgent spa treatments and contemporary German cuisine built around Bavarian flavours like venison, rabbit and lake trout as well as locally grown produce. Explore markets selling everything from Brotzeit to Bavarian blue cheese and of course Munich's beer gardens, home to the 'Big Six' breweries, baroque churches, parks and platzes. Through the winter, the Alpine valleys are blanketed in snow making for world class skiing and a truly festive holiday season atmosphere!

JUST GOA - IT'S INDIA, ONLY MORE SO!

Goa may be India's smallest state, but it is also amongst its most famous, for good reason too. Set on India's south west coast, Goa is known for its vast stretches of powder-puff sand thickly fringed by palm trees - a 95km coastline punctuated by some of the world's most beautiful beaches - Calangute, Colva, Cavelossim, Baga, Benaulim, Dona Paula and Palolem all giving onto the cerulean waters of the Arabian Gulf. But it's not just the beaches - venture inland and there are sanctuaries filled with wildlife, including tigers, elephants, Sambar deer and the iconic Malabar giant squirrel. Then there are the many festivals - from Diwali to Sao Jao with its colorful folk dances mixing local and Portuguese colonial influences. Karma Royal Haathi Mahal is just minutes walk from Cavelossim Beach and offers a cool sophisticated sanctuary featuring private apartments, a range of eating options including Atwood's Bar serving quintessential British offerings and Casa Lisboa serves local and international dishes in a Portuguese style setting.

PORTUGAL - SOUTHERN EUROPE WITH A TWIST...

The southernmost stretch of Portugal is probably its most famous region - the Algarve. It's the beaches and dramatic coastline that have made it famous of course and rightly so - think endless pretty bays of soft sand giving onto the deep blue waters of the Atlantic. But then there is the highly underrated cuisine, the local wines, the fresh seafood...Mediterranean influences abound but it is all unmistakably Portuguese, from the Pastéis de Bacalhau (codfish cakes) to Vinho Verde wines...Balaia Park is the perfect luxury residence during your stay, offering a host of villas and apartments plus a full complement of amenities from a range of restaurants, gym, golf and tennis, to spa, massage and even a Turkish bath!
