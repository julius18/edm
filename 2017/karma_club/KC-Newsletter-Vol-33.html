Explore the thermal lakes of Hungary at
Club DobogÓmajor Heviz

Not far from the thriving city of Budapest lie the city of Heviz and its accompanying lake. In the local parlance, 'Heviz' means 'cure water' - and as many locals will attest Lake Heviz cites a long history serving those suffering from rheumatic diseases and locomotive disorders, among others. Impressively, Lake Heviz is the second-largest thermal lake in the world, encompassing a surface area of 47,500 square metres.

As a Karma Club member, you have the opportunity to visit this beautiful and historical city on your next European tour, with a stay at the Club Dobogómajor resort, part of our global hospitality network. Spain's Costa del Sol; an Andalusian affair

Malaga, situated within Spain's famed Costa del Sol offers visitors to Spain a very Andalusian perspective of history and of Spain's ties to the Mediterranean Sea. A walk through the city's old centre tells the tale; the oldest architectural remains in the city are the walls of the Phoenician city, which are visible in the cellar of the Museo Picasso Málaga, while the Roman Amphitheatre, rediscovered in 1951, dates back to the 1st Century BC.

The internationally acclaimed painter and sculptor, Pablo Picasso, along with Hebrew poet and Jewish philosopher, Solomon Ibn Gabirol, and the actor Antonio Banderas were all born in Málaga.

Now, Karma Club members have the opportunity to explore this historically and culturally rich region for themselves with a stay at the Heritage Resorts Marbesa Club - a flexibly comfortable holiday-home style property right in the heart of the Costa del Sol and mere metres from the sandy Andalusian shores of the Med.
