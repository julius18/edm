Looking Forward to Oktoberfest
( 17th september - 3rd october 2016 )

In a little over two months, more than six million people will converge on the historic city of Munich in Bavaria, one of Germany's most picturesque regions, to celebrate the world famous Oktoberfest. With its Alpine peaks and placid lakes, Bavaria is a Mecca for nature lovers, skiers and trekking enthusiasts alike. But it is also the heartland of a unique cultural tradition in many ways unique from the rest of Germany - from its hearty rural cuisine (think Weisswurst sausages and Knödel dumplings) to its centuries old folk music to its iconic costumes (Lederhosen trousers for the men and Dirndl dresses for the women). And then of course there is the beer, one of the things for which Munich is famed around the world. Karma Bavaria is our stunning Alpine resort on the shores of Lake Schliersee, the perfect base for your Oktoberfest experience - plus our staff can give you the inside track on all the best brews, most conducive watering holes and country excursions.

3 Great Cities to Experience During your Bavaria Visit

The Medieval city of Munich, Bavaria's capital, is one of Germany's most culturally rich metropolises, home to the country's most famous football team (Bayern Munich), a wealth of stunning art and architecture and of course a myriad varieties of beer best sipped from steiners. Hop on a train from Munich and you can be in the Austrian capital of Vienna in as little as four hours. Known as the City of Music thanks to its rich classical heritage, it's also home to some of Europe's most refined Baroque architecture and is often sited for the quality of life it offers its 1.8 million citizens. Even closer is another great Austrian city - Salzburg, which is just a couple of hours away by train. Another shining example of gilded baroque architecture, Salzburg is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and was the birthplace of Mozart. Both cities offer a myriad of activities, from classical music concerts to beautifully curated museums to great shopping and dining.

SCILLY ON CELLULOID - OFFSHORE FILM FESTIVAL PUNCHES
ABOVE ITS WEIGHT

The Scilly Isles are Britain's best kept secret - set in the relatively balmy waters off England's southern coast just 15 minutes flight from Land's End, they could easily be mistaken for somewhere in the Mediterranean or even Pacific with their brilliant white beaches, turquoise waters and native palm trees. The proliferation of seals is a give away though! But besides its natural attributes, the island of St. Martin's also plays host to a film festival showcasing award winning new material, vintage movies and locally produced films over three days between 6th - 9th October. The festival is hosted at the Seven Stones Inn and is perhaps best enjoyed with some classic English ales either inside or on the sun-drenched terrace between screenings. Karma St. Martins is your gateway to the Scilly Isles, with its quaint cottage style accommodation, spa facilities, acclaimed restaurant and stunning views.
