PHUKET - THE QUINTESSENTIAL THAI ISLAND

Escape the winter blues with a revitalising visit to the legendary Thai Island of Phuket. There's a reason Phuket is world famous - in fact there are many! For one, there are those soft, white sand beaches studded with palm trees and lapped by turquoise waters. Then there are the fecund tropical forests of the interior - well worth a day trip with their astonishing biodiversity. Thai culture has to be among the most decorative and visually captivating in the world and it's on full display in Phuket - from the temples to the street food to the local dances, festivals and art. From Phuket's pretty old town to a boat ride around the stunning limestone karsts of the nearby Ao Phang Nga marine park. Karma Royal Phuket offers a wide range of apartments ideal for families or groups of friends - plus facilities like gym, games room, pool, Karma Spa, bakery and a restaurant serving excellent renditions of Thai and Italian cuisine. THE HEART OF SCOTLAND

Treat yourself to a short break in historic Scotland - The MacDonald Forest Hill Hotel & Spa just outside Stirling is a beautiful 4 star property appropriately set amid rolling, wooded hills. Situated in central Scotland, it's a perfect jumping off point for a Highland trip, or to the famous cities of Edinburgh and Glasgow - both richly endowed culturally, artistically and architecturally. And of course, a visit to Stirling Castle is a must. Visit the national William Wallace Monument, historic battlefields, Hadrian's Wall and wander among the magical lochs and mountains of its beautiful northern reaches. The resort itself features 1-3 bedroom accommodation ideal for couples and families alike - there's also a solarium, swimming pool, gym and of course spa.

ALL ROADS LEAD TO… ROME

When it comes to historically influential cities, few come close to Rome. A capital city to this day, Rome has been a dominant global force since the days of the Roman Empire. It is also the seat of the Roman Catholic church and the Vatican City - an independent state. All of these historic influences sit atop one another - walk round a corner in the city centre and you'll stumble up on the Pantheon - an incredible former Roman temple, now church, completed in the 1st century AD. Then there is the Colosseum, where gladiators did battle; much of it is still standing. Renaissance architecture jostles with these ancient artefacts and everywhere you'll find restaurants selling excellent Italian food and boutiques selling the sophisticated designer fashions and interior elements for which the country is famous. Carpe Diem Roma Golf Club is your gateway to the city - set in the surrounding countryside, it offers private residences for up to eight people as well as pool, bar, beach facilities and of course restaurant.
