Cornish Dreams


When you consider that the Scilly Isles are just a hop skip off the south coast of England, it's surprising that they are so little known. Especially since they are so special and unique - low slung islands of iridescent green, filled with wildlife, history and waters that almost resemble the Mediterranean. They lie just 28 miles from the tip of Land's End in Cornwall - not only the southernmost point of the UK, but also one of its prettiest destinations, famed for its spectacular coastal walks, wildlife and lighthouses. Well worth a stop off en route to the Scillies, which are easily accessed via a short flight from Newquay airport (www.newquaycornwallairport.com) - plus there are connections from major cities like London, Birmingham and Manchester. Karma St. Martin's, on the island of the same name, is your gateway to these sequestered and special little islands.

The fabled Cornish pasty & ice cream

Cornwall feels very different from the rest of England and its people are fiercely independent. They're also very proud of some of their iconic foodstuffs. Chief among these is surely the legendary Cornish pasty - a thick short crust pastry pocket filled with a rich blend of meats and vegetables, with a distinctive ridge. The latter once served a specific purpose - so that miners could easily clutch their lunch without getting coal all over it! Coming in a close second is Cornish ice cream whose incredible creaminess is thanks to copious amounts of delicious rich clotted cream. Both date back as far as the 13th century, so they've had a long time to get them just right! You'll find both easily available during your stay at Karma St. Martin's in the Isles of Scilly.
