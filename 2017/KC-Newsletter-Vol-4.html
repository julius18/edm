BALI’S MOST TREASURED BAY…

Jimbaran Bay in Bali is one of the island’s most celebrated destinations – the preserve of high end resorts and designer villas and home to a close knit local community intent on keeping their unique and fascinating culture intact. This is the setting for Karma Jimbaran, a boutique property of 38 luxury villas that spill down a gentle slope amidst vibrant tropical gardens. The soft white sand and unruffled waters of Jimbaran Bay, famed for its breathtaking sunsets, are just a short walk away. An early morning visit to the bustling Jimbaran fish market is a must – arrive in time for the offloading of the fresh catch, while the spectacular Uluwatu temple, famed for its sunset Kecak dance, is just a short drive away. Karma Jimbaran’s villas are inspired by the classic Balinese garden compound and feature a mix of designer comforts and amenities including top of the line audiovisual systems and appliances, all in beautiful spacious interiors featuring opulent furnishings and curated artworks from around Indonesia. You’ll love the airy but intimate Karma Lounge Restaurant and Veritas Duo wine bar, while Karma Spa is the perfect place to unwind after a day of discovering Bali’s many delights while your kids enjoy the facilities at the 3 Monkeys kids club.

CASTAWAY WITHOUT A CARE…

When you’re in need of an island escape that’s truly far from the madding crowd, a tiny archipelago of islands off the coast of Bali offers tropical treats for those in the know. The Gili Islands are tiny coral fringed atolls where neither dogs or motor vehicles are allowed, making them the islands of cats & bicycles. Gili Meno is among the most peaceful of them – think endless stretches of sugary white sand, reefs teeming with tropical fish and turtles, tiny homespun eateries serving fresh fish. Take an island hopping boat trip to the other Gili Islands including the biggest, Gili Trawangan which has more of a party reputation. Karma Reef offers castaway luxury with a medley of luxury beachfront Javanese lumbung’s and ‘glamping’ tents that are the perfect way to tune in with the tranquil natural environment, whilst enjoying all the amenities of a top hotel. Dive, snorkel, practice yoga or simply take some time out on your comfy personal sunbed …Karma Reef is all about ‘you’ time.

GOA: 5 OF THE BEST

Goa has long been a Karma Group mainstay, with its rich mix of colonial history, sophisticated culture, hedonistic vibes and world beating beaches. Here are five of our favourites while you’re visiting one of our award winning Goa resorts…

Mobor Beach
Check out this understated yet stunning spit of beautiful white sand between the Arabian Sea and the Assolna River Estuary. Bring a picnic and settle in for a tranquil treat.

Real Yoga
Complete your Goa experience with a calming yoga session in the very place it originated. There are countless retreats and studios to choose from – whether its vigorous vinyasa or tranquil yin you’re after.

Tiger Tiger
Discover Goa’s rich interior – home to a bewildering variety of flora & fauna. And chief amongst them is the biggest of the big cats – the tiger, which you can see in the wild cat reserve.

Old Goa
For 300 years and more, Old Goa was the Portuguese capital – celebrated around the world as a Venice of the east with its gothic cathedrals, grand colonial buildings and balmy atmosphere

Dive In
Miramar, about 3 kilometres from Panaji, may not be the prettiest of beaches, but head here if Scuba is your pleasure. Beneath the water, things are vibrant, colourful and brimming with life!
 

CRETE BEACHES: 5 OF THE BEST

Karma Royal Villea is your passport to one of southern Europe’s most historic destinations – and while you’ll marvel at the ancient remains, there are a myriad jaw dropping beaches for when you’re intent on some laid back down time. Here are five you won’t want to miss…

Balos Lagoon
Probably the most photographed beach in Crete – and for good reason. Iconic white sands meet turquoise waters in a stunning otherworldly environment

Glyka Nera
A pretty pebble beach giving onto deep blue waters with a dramatic backdrop of cliff faces – no wonder it has been ranked among the top 20 beaches in Europe.

Aspri Limni
This is a little known stretch of sand, southwest of Chania close to the ancient Chrysoksalitssa monastery. Don’t forget to visit the gorgeous Elafonissi lagoon nearby...

Loutro
The tiny seaside community of Loutro in Cape Mouri is believed to have been the site of the ancient city of Phoenix. The protected bay is home to a lovely sweep of sand and a natural harbour for boats.

Potamos
If you like your beaches on the wild side, this is the pick for you. Surrounded by tall cedars and evergreens, it is popular with campers – besides the beautiful beach itself, there’s a natural clay spa and you can even find fossils in the purling river.

KARMA ROYAL VILLEA

Your gateway to the historic island of Crete is Karma Royal Villea in the idyllic village of Makrigialos on Crete’s laid back southeastern coast lapped by the cerulean waters of the Aegean. Discover the islands timeless tavernas selling sweet heady drafts of local wine and its ancient ruins, once spectacular cities that were the foundation of western civilization...The resort boasts 70 beautifully appointed one bedroom studios, while you’ll find one of Crete’s many world beating beaches just 100 metres away. Whether it’s nature you want to discover, the fascinating millenia old culture or to bask in the Mediterannean medley of sunshine, delicous healthy food and cosmpolitan lifestyle, Crete is a constant revelation.
