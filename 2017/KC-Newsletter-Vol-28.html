CASTAWAY LUXURY JUST TWO HOURS SPEEDBOAT RIDE FROM BALI



Imagine the following: bone white beaches lapped by the Indian Ocean, horse-drawn carriages (known as cidomos) in place of cars, an ocean brimming with marine life including reef sharks and turtles… and a collection of luxury beachfront cabanas, villas and tents surrounded by palm trees. This is Karma Reef on the tiny island of Gili Meno. A mere two and a half hour speedboat ride from Bali, this idyllic island has long been a getaway favourite for travellers with a penchant for a castaway brand of barefoot luxury. Karma Reef offers just this kind of barefoot 5-star luxury. It features its own restaurant with fresh fish figuring prominently on the menu, a unique sea spa, sun loungers and a laid-back beach bar. If you can make it out of your hammock, head out on some island hopping trips and don't forget to bring your snorkel! Head over for the festive season and join the Jiggy Boat Party on December 17, which runs all afternoon.

FIND YOUR CENTRE AT THE BALI SPIRIT FESTIVAL 2017 IN UBUD

March 2017 is a big month in Ubud with the annual Bali Spirit Festival taking place between the 19th & 26th. The event runs for one week and bills itself as a 'global celebration of yoga, dance & music.' Many of the world's leading yoga teachers, therapists, healers, performers and exponents of world music converge on Ubud, Bali's cultural and artistic hub, with events taking place across a number of sites in the town. Arriving at the end of Bali's rainy season, this is the ideal time to experience the vibrancy and creative energy of this unique location. Stay at one of Karma's newest residences, Karma Mayura, an opulent resort complete with infinity pool, Karma Spa and spacious suites, set on the banks of a river in the pretty village of Mas on the outskirts of Ubud.

EXPLORE FiJi - THE VIBRANT HEART OF THE SOUTH PACIFIC

Fiji is among the most developed countries in the South Pacific - an archipelago of 330 islands that are spread across the 3rd largest barrier reef in the world (after the Great Barrier Reef and the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef). If you love the ocean and all it contains then you'll love Fiji - it boasts some of the highest levels of marine biodiversity on the planet. Add to that bone white beaches and a fascinating and welcoming local culture and you can see how it got its tropical paradise reputation. The Fiji Palms Resort nestles in a picturesque harbour just 50km from the capital Suva and brings together fully equipped apartment within easy reach of great restaurants and activities from river rafting to Jet Ski safaris to scuba diving and snorkeling.

DEEP SOUTH - NEW ZEALAND'S MAJESTIC WILDERNESS

New Zealand is one of the most isolated landmasses on earth giving it some utterly unique ecosystems and species. The South Island Te Waipounamu boasts some of the most spectacular natural features on the planet - a fact that has drawn filmmakers to use it as their mise en scene (it doubled for Middle Earth in Peter Jackson's Tolkien trilogy). Mount Hutt Lodge Resort is an hour's drive from Christchurch and sits on a hill with jaw dropping views of the surrounding mountains and river. The outdoor activities here are endless - from skiing in the winter to salmon fishing, trekking and golfing. Truly a special place under the sun.
