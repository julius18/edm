Culinary India - a food lovers’ journey

India may be known for the heat and spice of its cuisine, but what’s not commonly understood is how India’s food changes region by region. Karma’s chefs sum up three of the biggest food regions in a bite size chunk…

Goa - is known for its seafood and much of the State’s most famous dishes come from the sea.

Jaipur and Rajasthan - The desert and arid vistas of Rajasthan have imbued the region’s cuisine with a heat all their own.

Kerala - With its history as a melting pot of different religions (all with their own rules), Kerala’s cuisine features vegetarian delicacies served in ways that encourage sharing.

Hike the stunning Cook Islands

The Cook Islands, located in the South Pacific, are unlike anywhere else on earth. An idyllic climate, verdant landscape and friendly locals make the Cook Islands a perfect destination for lovers, families and adventure seekers of all ages. Those looking for a truly unique experience should try the cross-island walk; surely one of the world’s prettiest – and relatively easy – island hikes.

Stay at the Rarotongan Beach Resort and Spa, an all-in-one resort that’s built to impress with a pristine beach, luxury villas, salon, gym, spa and multiple restaurants serving contemporary island fare.

A Great Barrier Reef getaway

If you don’t have Australia’s Great Barrier Reef on your bucket list, put it on there! The Great Barrier Reef is one of the world’s great wonders – a living organism stretching 2,300 kilometres down the Queensland coast.

The Allegra Hervey Bay is the perfect launch point for a Great Barrier Reef adventure. While you’re there, head north to Bundaberg and take a tour of the famous Bundaberg Rum distillery.
