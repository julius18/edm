The Cloudesley Shovell Restaurant revels
in its Two Rosettes glory

Karma St Martin's signature restaurant, the Cloudesley Shovell, recently picked up a prestigious two Rosettes from the AA. Executive Chef, Mike Potts, led his talented culinary team to secure the award, which recognises the restaurant as the best on St. Martins. The team only use the freshest locally and ethically sourced produce to create delicious meals for our guests.

Arrive at Scilly in style via Island Helicopters

Island Helicopters is a is a new helicopter service running from Lands End to St Mary’s airport, connecting the Isles of Scilly to the mainland. This new addition to the Isles of Scilly transport network will operate year-round, with up to 8 flights a day, six days a week.

The best place to view the stars…

The lack of urbanisation on the Isles of Scilly - and subsequent lack of light pollution - makes them an ideal location for stargazing. Work has already started on a new observatory on St. Martins led by The Community Observatory St. Martins On Scilly (COSMOS). To raise the final £20,000 needed for COSMOS in time for Autumn, Karma St. Martin’s host a charity auction on Saturday 6th October 2018, with fantastic prizes going under the hammer for a good cause. Tantalisingly, a VIP guest will join in the fun.

Stay with us and save the sea

Karma St. Martin’s is serious about maintaining and protecting our pristine marine environment. Choose our “Good Karma” accommodation rate when booking 3 nights or longer, and we'll donate to two vital not-for-profit organisations: the National Lobster Hatchery, a leading regional marine conservation project, and the Royal National Lifeboat Institution (RNLI), a group of dedicated mariners who risk their lives to keep the seas safe, much like the Scilly's longboat pilots of yesteryear.
